# DPIA Pack — Structure & Rules

The structure for the DRAFT DPIA pack. It is a scaffold for the DPO to complete; it never rates risk or decides lawfulness, and it never contains personal data.

## Document structure

1. **Header / DRAFT line** — "DRAFT DPIA for <project> — generated <date>. Scaffold only; risk ratings, lawful basis, and sign-off are the DPO's, against the applicable law. Contains no personal data."
2. **Description of processing** — purposes, data categories, data subjects, recipients, transfers, retention. [TBC] where missing.
3. **Necessity & proportionality** — questions to answer (not answers).
4. **Risks to data subjects** — risks to consider, each as a prompt to assess.
5. **Risk table** — see below; rating cells blank.
6. **Mitigations to consider** — measures to evaluate.
7. **Open questions / [TBC]** — what the team must confirm.

## Risk table (rating cells stay blank)

| Risk to data subjects | Likelihood | Severity | Mitigation | Residual risk |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| [risk, phrased as a prompt to assess] | _(DPO)_ | _(DPO)_ | [measure to consider] | _(DPO)_ |

- Fill the Risk and Mitigation columns from the description.
- Leave Likelihood, Severity, and Residual risk blank — the DPO rates them.

## Never include

- A risk rating, residual-risk level, or DPIA outcome.
- The lawful basis stated as settled, or any "lawful / compliant / adequate" conclusion.
- Personal data of any kind — categories and purposes only.
- A decision on whether processing may proceed or requires prior consultation (flag high-risk factors for the DPO instead).

The assessment, the ratings, the lawful basis, and the sign-off are the DPO's, against the applicable law.
