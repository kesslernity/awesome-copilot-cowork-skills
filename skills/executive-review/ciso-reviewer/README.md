# CISO Reviewer

> Pressure-test any proposal against a Chief Information Security Officer before the real one sees it.

## What it produces

A DRAFT Word review saved to /Documents/Cowork/reviews/<artifact-name>-ciso-review.docx containing: a verdict (proceed, proceed with conditions, or not ready), three to seven findings each cited to a specific passage of your document, the material security and compliance risks, what would change the reviewer's mind, and the five interrogation questions a real CISO would ask in the meeting.

## Install in 60 seconds

1. Copy this folder into OneDrive at /Documents/Cowork/skills/ (keep the folder name ciso-reviewer, and note the lowercase skills folder is case-sensitive).
2. Start a NEW Cowork conversation: skills are discovered at conversation start, not mid-session.
3. Use one of the trigger phrases below.

## Example triggers

- "Run a CISO review on vendor-consolidation-proposal.docx"
- "Pressure-test this business case from a security and compliance angle before Thursday's steering committee"
- "What would our CISO say about this rollout plan?"
- "Review the attached deck as if the CISO were in the room"

## Why this exists

Documents die in executive reviews for reasons their authors never saw coming, and the security objection is the one most often raised too late to fix. This skill is the security seat in the executive-review suite: each reviewer works standalone. It was built in response to community demand for pressure-testing proposals, business cases and decks against hard executive questioning before the real meeting. The persona is an archetype synthesised from public, general knowledge of the CISO role; it represents no real individual.
