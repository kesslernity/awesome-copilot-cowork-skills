# CRO Reviewer

> A Chief Revenue Officer archetype that pressure-tests your proposal before the real one does.

## What it produces

A structured review document, saved as `<artifact-name>-cro-review.docx` in `/Documents/Cowork/reviews/` and labelled DRAFT, containing:

- VERDICT: proceed, proceed with conditions, or not ready
- TOP FINDINGS: each one quotes or cites the exact slide, page or section it is about
- RISKS: pipeline, sales motion, pricing, retention and forecast credibility risks
- WHAT WOULD CHANGE MY MIND: the specific evidence that would move the verdict
- 5 INTERROGATION QUESTIONS: what the real CRO would ask in the meeting, hardest first

## Install in 60 seconds

1. Copy this folder into your OneDrive at `/Documents/Cowork/skills/` (keep the folder name `cro-reviewer` and the lowercase `skills`).
2. Start a NEW Cowork conversation: skills are only discovered when a conversation starts.
3. Use one of the trigger phrases below.

## Example triggers

- "Review this business case as a CRO before I take it to the revenue leadership meeting."
- "Pressure-test q3-expansion-proposal.docx from a Chief Revenue Officer's point of view."
- "What would a CRO tear apart in this pricing deck?"
- "Run a CRO review on the partner programme plan in my OneDrive."

## Why this exists

This is one seat of the executive-review category: executive role archetypes that interrogate a document before it faces the real executives. The recurring ask behind the suite: a way to pressure-test proposals before real executive reviews, because the pipeline, pricing and forecast questions that sink business cases are predictable, and rehearsing against a sceptical revenue lens surfaces them while there is still time to fix the document. Each reviewer works standalone. Drop `org-profile.md` into `/Documents/Cowork/` (template in this skill's references folder) and every reviewer reads it as company context.
