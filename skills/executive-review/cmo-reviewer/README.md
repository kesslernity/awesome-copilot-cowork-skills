# CMO Reviewer

> A Chief Marketing Officer archetype tears your proposal apart on Tuesday, so the real one cannot on Thursday.

## What it produces

A DRAFT Word document, `<artifact-name>-cmo-review.docx`, saved to `/Documents/Cowork/reviews/` in your OneDrive, containing:

- A verdict: proceed, proceed with conditions, or not ready.
- Three to seven findings, each citing the exact slide, section or sentence it is about.
- Risks from the CMO lens: positioning, messaging risk, demand evidence, channel economics, launch readiness.
- What would change the reviewer's mind, stated as actionable items.
- The five interrogation questions the real executive would ask in the meeting.

If `/Documents/Cowork/org-profile.md` exists, the review uses it as company context; otherwise it runs generic and tells you so.

## Install in 60 seconds

1. Copy this folder (including `references/`) into your OneDrive at `/Documents/Cowork/skills/cmo-reviewer/`.
2. Start a NEW Cowork conversation (skills are discovered only when a conversation starts).
3. Use one of the trigger phrases below.

## Example triggers

- "Run a CMO review on Documents/Proposals/q3-campaign-business-case.docx"
- "Review this launch plan as a Chief Marketing Officer before I take it to the leadership meeting."
- "Pressure-test the positioning and pricing story in this deck like a CMO would."
- "What would a CMO tear apart in this go-to-market one-pager?"

## Why this exists

This skill is the CMO seat of the executive-review category: role archetypes that interrogate a document the way the real meeting will. The suite exists because of steady community demand for pressure-testing proposals, business cases and decks before real executive reviews: the hard questions are cheaper to hear from an archetype than from the executive who controls the budget. Each reviewer works standalone.
