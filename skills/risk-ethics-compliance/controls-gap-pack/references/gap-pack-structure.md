# Controls Gap Pack — Structure & Rules

The structure for the DRAFT controls-and-gap pack. It prepares a controls assessment; it never concludes compliance or control effectiveness.

## Document structure

1. **Header / DRAFT line** — "DRAFT controls & gap analysis for <requirement> — generated <date>. Apparent mapping only; whether obligations are met and controls are effective requires assessment and testing by control owners/audit. Not a compliance determination."
2. **Obligations & mapping table** — see below.
3. **Apparent gaps** — obligations with no/partial coverage.
4. **Questions for control owners** — existence, scope, operation, evidence.
5. **Actions to assess** — phrased "Assess whether…".
6. **Open questions / [TBC]**.

## Obligations & mapping table

| Obligation (from requirement) | Apparent control(s) | Coverage | Gap / question |
|---|---|---|---|
| [discrete obligation, traceable to the source] | [control from the provided descriptions, or "none provided"] | Addressed / Partial / Not evident | [gap or owner question] |

- Break the requirement into discrete, testable obligations.
- Map only controls the user provided; never invent one.
- Coverage is an **apparent** state for owners to confirm and test — never a verdict.

## Never include

- A statement that the organisation is compliant, or an obligation is "met".
- An assessment that a control is effective or operating.
- A risk-acceptance decision.
- Legal requirements asserted beyond the requirement's own text (flag jurisdiction instead).

Whether obligations are met and controls are effective is decided by control owners and internal audit, through assessment and testing — not by this pack.
